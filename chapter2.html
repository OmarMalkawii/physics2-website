Chapter 24: Gauss’s Law

Introduction [cite: 11]

Gauss’s Law provides a method to calculate electric fields using symmetry. It states that the total electric flux through a closed surface is equal to the charge enclosed divided by the permittivity of free space ($\epsilon_0$). [cite: 12]

Key Formulas [cite: 13]

  • $\Phi = q / \epsilon_0$ (Electric Flux $\Phi$, charge q, permittivity of free space $\epsilon_0$)
  • $\Phi = \oint E \cdot dA$ (Electric Flux as surface integral of Electric Field E over area A)
