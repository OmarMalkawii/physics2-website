Chapter 25: Electric Potential

Introduction [cite: 16]

Electric potential is the electric potential energy per unit charge at a point in an electric field. Itâ€™s a scalar quantity, unlike the electric field. Equipotential surfaces are surfaces where the electric potential is the same at every point. [cite: 17]

Key Formulas [cite: 18]

  • $V = U / q$ (Electric Potential V, Potential Energy U, charge q)
  • $V = k * q / r$ (Electric Potential due to a point charge)
  • $\Delta U = q * \Delta V$ (Change in Potential Energy)
  • $E = -dV / dx$ (Electric field as the negative gradient of potential, in 1D)
