Chapter 26: Capacitance and Dielectrics

Introduction [cite: 21]

Capacitance is the ability of a system to store electric charge per unit voltage. Dielectrics are insulating materials placed between the plates of a capacitor that increase its capacitance by reducing the effective electric field, which allows more charge to be stored at the same voltage. [cite: 22]

Key Formulas [cite: 23]

  • $C = Q / V$ (Capacitance C, Charge Q, Voltage V)
  • $C = \epsilon * A / d$ (Capacitance for parallel plates: permittivity $\epsilon$, Area A, distance d)
  • $U = 1/2 * C * V^2$ (Energy stored in a capacitor)
  • For a dielectric: $C' = \kappa * C_0$ (where $\kappa$ is the dielectric constant, $C_0$ is capacitance in vacuum)
  • $\epsilon = \kappa * \epsilon_0$
